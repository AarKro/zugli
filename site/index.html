Zügli sits in your home and watches the departures you care about. One line, a few, or your whole stop. It counts them down on a glowing LED panel, so you can see from across the room exactly when to leave. Live Swiss transit data, no app, built by you.

A single 64×64 panel, the same one Zügli lights up on your shelf.

Not every stop.
Not every platform.
Just the ones you run for.

Departure apps drown you in every line, every platform, every direction. Zügli does the opposite. Track the specific connections you care about at your stop, and it shows just those. The minutes until you need to leave, glowing on the shelf by your door.

Live data

Real Swiss times, refreshed every 30 seconds.

Zügli polls the official transport.opendata.ch API for your stop, keeps the next three departures you're tracking, and uses the real-time delayed time when there is one. The number on the panel is the number on the platform.

No app, no account

Set it up from your phone in two minutes.

On first power-up Zügli opens its own Zügli-Setup hotspot. Join it, pick your home WiFi, then open zugli.local and search for your stop. Choose whether to follow one connection, a few, or the whole stop, hit Save, and the panel switches over live, no reboot, nothing to install. Set the brightness or let it auto-dim at night from the same page.

Honest hardware

Open source, top to bottom.

An ESP32-S3 driving a single 64×64 HUB75 panel, firmware written in Rust (no_std, Embassy). Every wire, pin and line of code is published. Build it exactly as documented, or change whatever you like. It's yours.

Three steps to the shelf.

1

Connect

Join the Zügli-Setup hotspot and hand it your home WiFi password. It joins, then reboots onto your network.

2

Choose

Open zugli.local, search your stop, and pick the connections you ride: one, a few, or the whole stop. Hit Save and the panel switches over live.

3

Watch

Set it on a shelf by the door. From now on it counts down your next departures, and you leave at the right minute, every time.

What's inside.

Five off-the-shelf parts. No custom boards and no soldering, just Dupont wires and a screwdriver.

ESP32-S3-DevKitC

The brain. WiFi, Rust firmware, drives the panel.

64×64 HUB75 panel

The display. A bright copper-on-black LED matrix.

5 V / 5 A power supply

Feeds the panel directly, with headroom for full brightness.

PSU adapter

Screw-terminal pigtail from supply to panel lugs.

Dupont cables

14 signal lines + ground, ESP32 to panel header.

That's the whole list.

Five parts, no custom hardware, nothing exotic.
